Ports Supported in Mixed-Language Design (Verilog and VHDL)

For a mixed Verilog-VHDL design, the following port connections are allowed at the partition boundary:

  • VHDL std_logic and std_logic_vector
  • Verilog net and variable
  • Verilog constant expression, bit-select, part select, concatenation
  • VHDL constant expression, array elements, array slices

When Verilog instantiates VHDL, a Verilog variable cannot be connected to a VHDL out or inout port (error DSSRGC). When VHDL instantiates Verilog, the underlying Verilog port cannot be a variable port (error DSSVRG).

Other limitations of mixed-language designs are:

  • Connections between unresolved and resolved types are not allowed.
  • Instantiation of a Verilog model in VHDL using a VHDL shell, or instantiation of a VHDL model in Verilog using a Verilog shell, is not supported. The elaborator generates an INCVLG error.
